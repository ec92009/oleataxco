# Olea Tax Co Website PRD

## Document Status

- Status: Draft
- Product: Olea Tax Co website
- Product type: Marketing and lead-generation site
- Current preview: `https://ec92009.github.io/Codex/oleataxco/`
- Local workspace: `~/Codex/web/github.io/oleataxco`

## 1. Product Summary

Olea Tax Co needs a professional website that positions the firm as a strategic CPA partner for business owners, real estate investors, and high-income families. The website should communicate that the firm offers proactive tax planning, not just annual filing, and should convert qualified visitors into consultation requests.

The current site is a draft marketing homepage with placeholder booking UI and draft copy. This PRD defines the requirements for a production-ready website that can launch publicly on a simple stack first and later move into SquareSpace or another CMS if needed.

## 2. Problem

Many potential clients see CPAs as reactive, transactional, and only relevant during tax season. That makes it harder for Olea Tax Co to stand out as a high-trust advisory practice focused on year-round planning and strategic decision support.

The current draft site does a reasonable job of introducing the offer, but it still contains placeholder elements, incomplete trust signals, and non-final conversion paths. A clearer product definition is needed so design, copy, and launch decisions stay aligned.

## 3. Goals

- Position Olea Tax Co as a proactive tax strategy firm rather than a filing-only service.
- Attract qualified leads from the right client profiles.
- Build trust quickly through positioning, founder credibility, and process clarity.
- Make service packages understandable without requiring a sales call first.
- Create a clear primary conversion path for booking or inquiry.

## 4. Non-Goals

- Full client portal or document-sharing application.
- Online payment or self-serve checkout.
- Complex scheduling workflows in the first release.
- Extensive content marketing or blog system at launch.
- Broad consumer tax marketplace positioning.

## 5. Target Audience

### Primary audience

- Business owners with increasing tax complexity
- Real estate investors with growing portfolios or entity structures
- High-income families needing coordinated tax strategy

### Secondary audience

- Early-stage entrepreneurs likely to grow into advisory clients
- Referral partners evaluating Kelly as a specialist
- Local prospects looking for a more personal CPA relationship

## 6. User Needs

- Understand what Olea Tax Co does within the first screen
- Know whether the firm is the right fit for their situation
- See how strategic planning differs from basic tax filing
- Understand available service packages and what each includes
- Learn how the working relationship will feel and function
- Book a call or contact Kelly without friction

## 7. Value Proposition

Olea Tax Co helps business owners, investors, and high-income households make better tax decisions year-round through proactive planning, direct advisory access, and structured execution.

## 8. Positioning

### Core positioning statement

Strategic CPA advisory for clients who want tax planning integrated into business and financial decisions, not treated as a once-a-year filing event.

### Messaging pillars

- Proactive planning over reactive filing
- Direct relationship with Kelly
- Thoughtful structure for increasingly complex financial situations
- Boutique, high-touch service model

## 9. Success Metrics

### Primary metrics

- Consultation request rate
- Qualified lead volume
- Click-through rate on primary CTA
- Percentage of visitors reaching service and booking sections

### Secondary metrics

- FAQ engagement
- Scroll depth through founder and service sections
- Contact action split between booking, email, and phone
- Mobile conversion rate

## 10. Product Principles

- Trust first: the site should feel credible, calm, and high-signal.
- Clarity over cleverness: every section should reduce uncertainty.
- Quality over volume: attract the right clients, not all clients.
- Human over corporate: Kelly’s presence should feel direct and personal.

## 11. Information Architecture

The production site should include these sections in this order:

1. Header and navigation
2. Hero
3. Value/outcomes section
4. Founder section
5. Service packages
6. Process
7. Qualification / fit section
8. Booking or inquiry section
9. FAQ
10. Contact section

Future optional pages:

- About
- Services detail pages
- Contact
- Resources or checklist download

## 12. Functional Requirements

### 12.1 Header and navigation

- Clear brand display
- Short positioning line under or near brand
- Navigation to services, founder/about, process, FAQ, and booking
- Primary CTA visible in header

### 12.2 Hero

- Headline must clearly differentiate Olea Tax Co from filing-only firms
- Supporting paragraph must reinforce year-round strategic value
- Immediate trust indicators should appear near the headline
- Primary CTA and secondary CTA must both be visible

### 12.3 Founder section

- Introduce Kelly’s background and philosophy
- Explain why the firm exists and how the service model differs
- Use founder image and concise but credible bio

### 12.4 Services section

- Present 3 clear service packages
- Describe audience, inclusions, and outcome for each package
- Make it obvious that packages increase in strategic involvement

### 12.5 Process section

- Show a simple multi-step workflow
- Reduce ambiguity around onboarding and ongoing support
- Reinforce structure and professionalism

### 12.6 Qualification / fit section

- Help visitors self-select
- Signal that the firm is best for advisory-minded clients
- Support premium positioning without sounding exclusionary

### 12.7 Booking / inquiry section

- Include one primary lead path at launch
- If scheduler is not live, replace placeholder UI with a real inquiry path
- If scheduling is live, it must feel complete and trustworthy

### 12.8 FAQ section

- Answer high-friction questions before contact
- Prioritize remote service, catch-up work, one-time services, and fit

### 12.9 Contact section

- Must include final email and/or phone details
- Buttons must be functional, not placeholder links
- Messaging should invite the right kind of inquiry

## 13. Content Requirements

### Required launch content

- Final headline and subhead
- Final positioning line under brand
- Final founder bio
- Final package names and descriptions
- Final CTA copy
- Final FAQ copy
- Real contact information

### Recommended trust content

- CPA credentials and years of experience
- Service area
- Remote-work statement
- Industries or client types served
- Response expectation or intake process

### Open dependencies

- Kelly’s final copy approval
- Final phone/email/contact method
- Decision on whether booking is real or inquiry-based
- Any legal/compliance copy needed for launch

## 14. Visual and Brand Requirements

- Warm, premium, and personal tone
- Strong readability on desktop and mobile
- Clear distinction between primary actions and secondary actions
- Visual hierarchy should support trust, not novelty for its own sake
- Founder-led and boutique, not generic corporate accounting

## 15. MVP Scope

### In scope

- Single homepage
- Clear packages
- Founder section
- Process section
- FAQ
- Working CTA path
- Mobile-ready responsive design

### Out of scope

- Blog
- Resource library
- Client portal
- Dynamic quote flow
- Multi-page service detail architecture

## 16. Risks

- Placeholder content can undermine trust if it remains too long
- A fake scheduler can hurt conversion more than help it
- If positioning becomes too broad, the site may attract the wrong leads
- If positioning becomes too narrow, the lead pool may be unnecessarily constrained
- Without concrete trust builders, premium advisory positioning may feel unsupported

## 17. Assumptions

- Olea Tax Co wants to prioritize strategic advisory clients over commodity tax-prep traffic
- The current 3-package model is directionally correct but may still evolve
- Kelly will refine the detailed package copy and contact flow before launch
- The current homepage can serve as the MVP public site if placeholder elements are resolved

## 18. Recommended Next Steps

1. Finalize Kelly’s decisions on target clients, brand voice, and CTA path.
2. Replace all placeholder or draft-only elements with real launch-ready copy and links.
3. Decide whether the primary conversion should be booking, contact form, phone, or email.
4. Add final trust builders such as credentials, service area details, and response expectations.
5. Prepare a launch checklist for content QA, mobile QA, and contact-flow testing.
